George Herbert Mead

Philosopher, Sociologist, Psychologist

Background

Full Name: George Herbert Mead

Born: February 27, 1863 (Massachusetts)

Died: April 26, 1931 (Chicago)

Nationality: American

Early Life & Career

Father was a minister, mother was a teacher. Studied at Oberlin and Harvard, later influenced by European philosophy in Germany.

In 1894, he joined University of Chicago where he taught philosophy.

Contributions

Founder of Symbolic Interactionism.

Focused on development of the self, concepts of “I” and “Me,” and the “Generalized Other.”

Legacy

Shaped modern sociology, social psychology, and education.

His ideas continue to influence identity, communication, and social behavior studies.

Stages of Self-Development

Preparatory Stage (0–2 yrs): Imitation without understanding.

Play Stage (2–6 yrs): Role play, pretending to be others.

Game Stage (7+ yrs): Understands multiple roles and group rules.

The “I” and the “Me”

The “Me”: The socialized self, shaped by society’s rules and expectations.

The “I”: The creative, spontaneous self that expresses individuality.
